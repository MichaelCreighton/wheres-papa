Papa's Story

More than 30 years of running on hard surfaces, in the days before running shoes were highly engineered, gave me good cardio, but it also gave me arthritis in both feet. Bottom line is, I can no longer run, but I can still walk, and I walk a lot.

I’m an early riser, so most days, I don’t have anyone to talk to during my walk, which means I end up with lots of time to think. During one of my 2 ½ hour walk/think sessions, it occurred to me that there might be a way to leverage what I’m already doing, to generate money for a worthwhile cause. That’s where the idea of walking across Canada, from Vancouver to Halifax, originated. For a brief moment, I had thought of actually walking the roads/highways, but the logistics of making that work, quickly put an end to that idea and the project reverted back to a virtual journey.

Which cause to support was an easy decision for me. I’m the very proud grandfather of a Special Olympics Athlete (Brett) and I know from firsthand experience, the amazing work that organization does. I also know the constant struggle they face to find funding for their programs.

Although Special Olympics programs focus on sports and fitness, they are much more than that. The movement’s true power lies not only in its ability to create change in the lives of the children, youth and adults registered in their programs, but also in the communities in which they live. For people with an intellectual disability, Special Olympics is often the only place where they have an opportunity to develop a strong belief in themselves. Through Special Olympics, they begin to see themselves differently. Whether by stepping onto the ice or onto the track, they now see themselves as athletes, who can do so much more. As a spokesperson for Special Olympics, my grandson Brett is a shining example of the success of their programs (click here for Brett’s story).

If this fundraising effort turns out to be successful and I’m able to maintain my health, this will be the first leg of a four year, four leg tour of North America. Legs, 2, 3 and 4, would be from Halifax to Miami, then Miami to Los Angeles and finally, from Los Angeles back to Vancouver, the original starting point.

So that’s how I got here and why I’m doing this. Hopefully you can help me, help these exceptional Special Olympics athletes. They don’t make pennies anymore, but even if you were to pledge a single one of those now obsolete pennies per mile/kilometer, it would really help. Brett and I both urge you to consider making that minimum pledge. It’s important!
